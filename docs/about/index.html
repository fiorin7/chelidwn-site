How It All Happened

It all started in 2012 when five college students (pictured on the left) tired of carrying huge dictionaries around came together to try and make a digital version of the university Klingon dictionary. Once it was finished it served well not only us but also other students and professors alike.

Soon we all became rich in various ways - three of us had become stuffed animals seller and the other two were doing youtube as a job. We used the money to found our small association for digitization of classical materials.

Since then we managed to finish numerous projects in different humanitarian fields and our team grew threefold, including Matusek, my only consolation at eleven pm making this site.

Our Mission

  • We're trying to provide a more affordable and more accessible alternative to traditional paper humanitarian research resources.
  • Our projects aim to include professionals with both humanitarian and IT background in order to produce results that neither side can produce on its own.
  • We aim to provide a platform for translators and lexicographers to publish their work.

Current Projects

Optimization of our dictionaries' search engine

We've published a new version of our search engine in 2018. Since then we've been trying to optimize it even more so that no lemmas are missed during search

Addition of missing lemmas in our dictionaries

After our kind readers pointed out that there were some lemmas missing in two of our dictionaries, we started gradually including them.

Proofing of OCR Materials - We need help from you!

Despite using the highest quality OCR software, we still need someone to help us go over the texts themselves. If you're a university student, your university will count your help as taking part in an official internship here.
